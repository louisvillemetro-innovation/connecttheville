 Today, participation in society relies on the ability to access and use the internet effectively. Whether applying for a job, doing homework, or starting a business, all our residents need digital skills, tools, and connectivity to fully participate in modern society. These factors are increasingly becoming the choke points that prevent thousands of our citizens from adapting to and accessing the benefits of a knowledge-driven, innovative economy.

In 2017, Mayor Fischer and Louisville Metro released the city's first Digital Inclusion Plan, a strategic document highlighting the importance of addressing the digital divides within our communities and laying out action steps to do so. The plan outlines three major areas for digital inclusion: connectivity, helping citizens get connected to the internet; hardware, making sure citizens have computers, not just mobile devices, for full access to online resources; and skills, giving citizens the tools to be able to use the internet to improve their lives.

To learn more about Louisville Metro's digital inclusion initiatives and to download the full Digital Inclusion Plan, please visit digitalinclusion.louisvilleky.gov
